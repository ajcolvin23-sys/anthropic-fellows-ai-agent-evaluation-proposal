# AI Agents in Small-Organization Workflows

This repository contains an optional research proposal prepared by Alfred Colvin for the Anthropic Fellows Economics & Policy context.

## Project Focus

The proposal asks:

> How do AI agents affect productivity, decision quality, and operational risk in small businesses, churches, nonprofits, and community organizations?

Small organizations often adopt AI tools before they have formal evaluation, compliance, or safety-review processes. This project frames that gap as a practical research opportunity: AI agents may expand capacity and improve speed, but they may also introduce accuracy risk, overreliance, poor communication, or unclear accountability.

## Proposed Evaluation

The proposal outlines a workflow benchmark across use cases such as:

- Lead follow-up
- Grant readiness
- Marketing campaign drafting
- Client communication
- Research summarization
- Internal task routing

It compares human-only, AI-drafted, and AI-plus-human-review workflows across time saved, output quality, accuracy, risk flags, human-review burden, and deployment readiness.

## Positioning

This is a research-proposal artifact, not a claim of completed empirical research. It is intended to show applied AI evaluation thinking, policy relevance, workflow-design judgment, and attention to responsible deployment in real-world organizations.
